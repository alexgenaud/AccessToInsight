The Pali Canon

On-line editions

  • Ancient Buddhist Texts <http://www.ancient-buddhist-texts.net/> (Ven. Anandajoti Bhikkhu) offers a number of English translations of suttas and Vinaya texts, some line-by-line Pali-English translations (a useful aid to Pali students), and several metrical analyses of Pali and Sanskrit verse forms.
  • Digital Pali Reader <http://sourceforge.net/projects/digitalpali/> (Ven. Yuttadhammo) is an extraordinary and highly recommended cross-platform tool for Pali students of every level. Allows intermediate Pali students to read the Pali Canon. Automatically recognizes Pali words and gives definitions from the CPED and PED, as well as DPPN if available. Includes text search and dictionary lookup.
  • SuttaCentral's Online Sutta Correspondence Project <http://www.suttacentral.net/> enables one to identify the Chinese, Tibetan, and Sanskrit "parallels" or "counterparts" to the suttas of the four main Pali Nikayas — and vice-versa.
  • Thai Tipitaka <http://www.bhodhiyana.org/tipitaka_library.html> (Bhodhiyana Meditation Center) offers the Thai Tipitaka (in Thai script).
  • The Tipitaka <http://www.metta.lk/tipitaka/> (Mettanet, Sri Lanka) offers the romanized Pali version of the Sri Lanka Buddha Jayanti Tipitaka.

CD-ROM editions

  • BUDSIR — Buddhist Scriptures Information Retrieval <http://www.budsir.org> (Mahidol University, Thailand) is a CD-ROM containing the Thai edition of the Pali Tipitaka and Atthakatha (commentaries) in romanized script. Cost: about US$300.
  • Chattha Sangayana (Sixth Council) Tipitaka CD <http://www.vridhamma.org/Chattha-Sangayana-CD-ROM-Update> (Vipassana Research Institute, India) is a CD-ROM containing the Chattha Sangayana (Sixth Council, 1954-1956) edition of the Tipitaka, transcribed from the Myanmar print edition. It also includes the Tipitaka, Atthakatha (commentaries), and Tika and AnuTika (sub-commentaries), all displayed in seven scripts, including Roman and Devanagari. Cost: varies according to shipping destination.

Pali fonts  

  • Gentium <http://scripts.sil.org/Gentium_download> is a free Unicode font that is both elegant and pleasantly readable both on-screen and in print.
  • PaliPad <http://palipad.googlecode.com/git/palipad.html> (Chew Boon Aik) is a handy Javascript tool that converts Velthuis diacritic text into its Unicode equivalent.
  • Romanized Pali Transliteration Conversion <http://www.genaud.net/2013/03/convert/> (Alexander Genaud) is a handy online tool that converts Romanized Pali between its Velthuis, Unicode, and ASCII equivalents.
  • Sanskrit Diacritical Fonts for the Macintosh <http://www.columbia.edu/~ph2046/RnD/Hackett/Sanskritfonts.html> (Columbia University) offers Pierre Robillard's DPalatino, an excellent diacritic font (only for Mac) that has been used for many years by Wisdom Publications in such books as The Long Discourses of The Buddha, The Middle Length Discourses of the Buddha, and The Connected Discourses of the Buddha.
  • Softerviews <http://www.softerviews.org/Fonts.html> offers several Unicode fonts suitable for working with Pali.
  • Transliteration and Devanagari Fonts for Sanskrit <http://www.sanskritweb.net/fonts/> (Ulrich Stiehl) has an extensive review of available Pali and Sanskrit fonts, including "URW Palladio", a font custom designed to meet the Buddhist Publication Society's publishing requirements.
  • Transliteration Fonts for Mac users <http://imp.lss.wisc.edu/~gbuhnema/macfonts.html> (University of Wisconsin) offers K.R. Norman's MyTymes and Normyn fonts.

Pali language dicussion forums  

  • The Pali Collective <http://groups.yahoo.com/group/Pali/>, a Yahoo! group, has some very knowledgeable and helpful members who can help you with your Pali studies. A most valuable resource.

Pali language learning resources  

  • Elementary Pali Course by Ven. Narada Thera is available from several sources:
    • BuddhaNet (downloadable PDF document) <http://www.buddhanet.net/ebooks_s.htm>
    • Pratyeka (downloadable PDF document) <http://www.pratyeka.org/narada>. This version contains many corrections that are not present in other online transcriptions.
    • Tipitaka.net (on-line edition) <http://www.tipitaka.net/pali/>
  • How to input/type Romanized Pali and Sanskrit in Openoffice 3 <http://thanhsiang.org/faqing/node/24> (Faqing)
  • Lessons in Language Pali <http://buddhism.lib.ntu.edu.tw/BDLM/en/lesson/pali/lesson_pali1.htm> (Digital Library & Museum of Buddhist Studies, Taiwan) includes a word-by-word grammatical analysis of the entire Dhammapada.
  • The Online Pali Tutor <http://www.arrowriver.ca/pali/paliDrill1.html> (Arrow River Forest Hermitage, Canada) is an interactive tool for testing your knowledge of noun declensions, vocabulary, and more.
  • Pali-English Dictionary <http://dsal.uchicago.edu/dictionaries/pali/index.html> (PTS & U. of Chicago, USA), the best Pali-English Dictionary around, is now available online.
  • Pali Language Tools <http://nibbanam.com/pali_language_tools.html> (Nibbāna Mediation Association) has some interesting software tools and resources useful for anyone studying the Pali Canon, including word lists of Pali vocabulary.
  • The Pali Primer <http://www.vridhamma.org/Pali-Primar-Online> (VRI) Lily de Silva's excellent brief introductory textbook to Pali.
  • Pali Text Reader <http://palireader.sourceforge.net/> (Sourceforge; Lennart Lopin) is a feature-rich open source software tool for reading and studying the Pali Canon. A free mobile version is also available.
  • Pali Workshop <http://www.audiodharma.org/teacher/221/> (Sean Kerr) is a series of audio recordings from a Pali language workshop.
  • Pariyatti Learning Center <http://learning.pariyatti.org/> (Pariyatti) is an on-line Pali training and resource center.
  • Resources for Learning Pali <http://www.pratyeka.org/pali/> (Eisel Mazard) offers information for Pali students interested in reading the language in non-Roman scripts (Sinhalese, Burmese, and Muul-Khmer). Also offers a PDF edition of Duroiselle's A Practical Grammar of the Pali Language <http://www.pratyeka.org/duroiselle/>.
  • Transliteration and Devanagari Fonts for Sanskrit <http://www.sanskritweb.net/fonts/> (Ulrich Stiehl) has an extensive review of Pali and Sanskrit fonts.
  • The Words of the Buddha <http://www.buddha-vacana.org/> (owner unknown) offers a number of suttas in romanized Pali, side-by-side with their English translations. In some suttas, if you hover the mouse over a Pali word a literal translation of that word appears.
  • The University of Minnesota (USA) maintains a searchable list of institutions in North America offering academic courses in "Less Commonly Taught Languages" <http://www.carla.umn.edu/lctl/db/> (including Pali).
